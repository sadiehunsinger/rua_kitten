About Baby Kitten

Terris and I fell in love with this tiny, 7-pound baby kitten from the moment we met her at the Denver Dumb Friend's League. She wasn't a baby kitten, of course, she was 9 years old and had just had dental surgery. But she wasn't afraid of us. She came straight to us and was so curious about the room. She let us pick her up and hold her. She was the third cat we saw that day. And the last.

Naming Baby Kitten

We both loved the sound of Scottish names, and since we both have Scottish ancestry (Terris much more strongly than me), we decided that we were going to find the perfect Scottish name for her. After multiple translations, Rua - for red - stuck. We couldn't leave it at that single-word name, though. Her full name is Madam President Rua, Princess of Purth. She's a President and a Princess. It's a thing.

Taking Care of Baby Kitten

Rua is basically the best creature in the entire world, but her bodily functions have been less than pleasant. When we first got her, she loved to pee on soft things. The vet called it "a substrate preference". Thankfully just getting her a second litter box did the trick. Vomit was a continuous problem for a while. And poop. A lot of both because the food we were giving her must have been shit or her body isn't equipped to digest it. It went straight through her. The poor thing. So we switched from a dry food to a wet food, and the vomiting has essentially disappeared. Her poop though, my god. She poops less but it stinks more. She also pees on the wall behind her litter box. It's like she has no sense of where her pee is spraying, so it often gets in the crack of the baseboard. It's pretty lovely. Not as lovely as her, though, of course.
